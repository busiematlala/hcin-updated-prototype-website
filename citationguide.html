IIE Harvard Anglia In-Text Citation Examples

In-text citations acknowledge the sources used within academic writing. The examples below demonstrate common citation formats used in the IIE Harvard Anglia Referencing Style.

Single Author

Narrative Citation

Rogers (2023) states that usability plays an important role in interactive system design.

Parenthetical Citation

Usability plays an important role in interactive system design (Rogers, 2023).

Two Authors

Narrative Citation

Rogers and Preece (2023) explain that usability goals improve the user experience.

Parenthetical Citation

Usability goals improve the user experience (Rogers and Preece, 2023).

Three or More Authors

Narrative Citation

Rogers et al. (2023) argue that interaction design should focus on user needs.

Parenthetical Citation

Interaction design should focus on user needs (Rogers et al., 2023).

Organisation as Author

Narrative Citation

World Health Organization (2023) highlights the importance of global health initiatives.

Parenthetical Citation

Global health initiatives remain important (World Health Organization, 2023).

Direct Quotation

According to Rogers (2023, p.45), "usability is a fundamental aspect of successful interaction design."

No Author

When no author is available, use the title in place of the author.

(Guide to Referencing, 2023)

Secondary Citation

Smith (2018 cited in Rogers, 2023) argues that user-centred design improves usability.

Multiple Sources Supporting the Same Point

Several studies have shown that usability improves learning (Rogers, 2023; Preece, 2022; Sharp, 2021).

Page Numbers in Direct Quotations

Direct quotations should always include a page number.

"Users should be at the centre of the design process" (Rogers, 2023, p.67).

Common In-Text Citation Mistakes

  • Forgetting to include the publication year.
  • Including a URL inside an in-text citation.
  • Using page numbers when paraphrasing.
  • Using "et al." incorrectly.
  • Not matching in-text citations with the reference list.
  • Using direct quotations without page numbers.

Why In-Text Citations Matter

In-text citations give credit to the original author and help readers locate the full source within the reference list.

Correct use of citations strengthens academic integrity and helps students avoid plagiarism.
