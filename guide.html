Common IIE Harvard Anglia Reference Formats

Use the examples below as a guide when creating reference list entries. These examples follow the IIE Harvard Anglia Referencing Style.

Book

Rogers, Y., Sharp, H. and Preece, J., 2023. Interaction design: beyond human computer interaction. 6th ed. Wiley.

E-Book

Rogers, Y., Sharp, H. and Preece, J., 2023. Interaction design: beyond human computer interaction. [e-book] Available at: <https://www.example.com> [Accessed 17 May 2026].

Website

World Health Organization, 2015. WHO calls on countries to protect health from climate change. [online] Available at: <https://www.who.int> [Accessed 17 May 2026].

Journal Article

Davis, C., 2014. 'The aims of research', Journal of Research, 42(1), pp.7–14.

Newspaper Article

Smith, J., 2024. 'Universities embrace artificial intelligence'. The Times, 15 April, p.6.

YouTube Video

Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs, 2007. Sustainable development: the bigger picture. [video online] Available at: <https://www.youtube.com> [Accessed 17 May 2026].

Podcast

Yesterday in Parliament, 2015. BBC Parliament Podcast. [podcast] Available at: <http://www.bbc.co.uk> [Accessed 17 May 2026].

Blog Article

Brown, T., 2024. 'Understanding academic referencing'. [blog] Available at: <https://www.exampleblog.com> [Accessed 17 May 2026].

PDF Document

Department of Health, 2008. Health inequalities: progress and next steps. [pdf] Available at: <https://www.choiceforum.org/docs/inequalities.pdf> [Accessed 17 May 2026].

Important Referencing Reminders

  • Always list sources alphabetically in the reference list.
  • Use italics for book titles, journal titles and report titles.
  • Include access dates for all online sources.
  • Ensure punctuation matches the required format.
  • Check author names carefully.
  • Use the correct source type when generating references.
  • Consult the In-Text Citation Guide for citation examples.

Purpose of the Reference Guide

The purpose of this guide is to help students understand the structure and formatting of references used in academic assignments.

The examples provided allow students to compare their references against correctly formatted examples and improve their understanding of the IIE Harvard Anglia Referencing Style.
